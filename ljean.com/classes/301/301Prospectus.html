Overview:
Security and privacy are technologies of control. Understanding security and privacy does not require understanding cryptography. It does, however, require an understanding of the fundamental policy choices about surveillance, identity and information control. There are dangerous common practices (even after reformatting all the data remains on disposed hard drives) and useful myths (turn off your computer each night). The information sent to software providers may be a personal annoyance or a threat to civil liberties depending on the machine. Falsified email may be an annoyance or a threat. Understand the threats to and protections of privacy and security in the information realm. With this understanding, learn to manage secure valuable information.

Course Topics

Overview
A consideration of the current virus including the administrative email, the fake Microsoft patch and SoBig. What are they and how do they spread?

Why Digital Is Different

Economic and system arguments for why network security is distinct from other security problems.

War and Terror on the Internet
The role of the Internet in the first and second gulf wars. Emerging "network" terror groups.

Examples and Cases
Two distinct case studies that together reinforce, as practical examples, the distinct issues with digital networked security. The topics will be digital voting and hacking. With respect to hacking, the course will focus on evaluating a particular espionage incident.

Trusted Computing
Trusted computing -- when trust is used to support or subvert stated policy. How to distinguish trustworthy computing that requires that you trust some other party, and trustworthy computing that empowers the user.

Network Privacy and Data Protection
Privacy with a focus on "wire-tapping the Internet." Government surveillance initiatives including the FBI's Carnivore, NATO's Echelon, and Total Information Awareness by the Department of Defense. Private sector use of personal data.

Risks in surveillance technology.
Economic, political and technical risks of surveillance. Privacy issues in emerging technology, including tiny embedded wireless devices for clothing and accessories.

Authentication
Identity technologies including Liberty and Passport. The myths and reality of biometrics.

The Human in the Loop
Interaction with technology, a consideration of interfaces and trust. "Human engineering" is the best way to break into systems.

Plagues of the Network
Viruses and spam depend on the same human behaviors and economic foundations.

Cryptography
A technology and policy overview, including the technical results of recent policies. More on the technology, including digital signatures and secret sharing. A particular focus on recognizing the hallmarks of inferior products.

Closing and Cases
An examination of commonly installed hostile software. Best management practices, closing discussions.
